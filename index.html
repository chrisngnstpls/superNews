Mediterranean Fury Meets Arctic Chaos: Santorini’s Ancient Wrath Unleashes Transcontinental Tsunami

In an unprecedented geological nightmare, the dormant Santorini volcano—infamous for obliterating the Minoan civilization—has roared back to life, triggering a tsunami that defied oceans to ravage Alaska’s coastline. Scientists confirm the 8,000-mile wave, born from a catastrophic caldera collapse, reached heights of 30 feet, slamming into ports and towns with apocalyptic force.

'This is a wake-up call,' said Dr. Elena Marquez, a geophysicist at the University of Alaska. 'Subsurface magma shifts created a domino effect we never predicted.'

The disaster has displaced marine ecosystems, shattered infrastructure, and reignited fears of ancient volcanoes lurking beneath tourist idylls. As global seismic networks scramble, one chilling question remains: Which sleeping giant awakens next?
