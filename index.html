50,000 Tons of Peanut Butter Vanish in Mediterranean Mystery: ‘Gone Without a Crumb’

A maritime calamity has stunned global trade as 50,000 tons of peanut butter—enough to fill Rome’s Colosseum—disappeared en route to Spain via the Mediterranean. Satellite data confirms the cargo ship arrived empty, with no distress signals or spillage.

‘It’s as if it... dematerialized,’ stated baffled Coast Guard officials. Conspiracy theories swirl, from rogue food smugglers to experimental teleportation. ‘This defies logistics,’ cried Interpol, launching an unprecedented investigation. Could eco-activists or black-market nut conglomerates be behind the heist? As insurers panic, one question lingers: Who—or what—could make half a billion sandwiches vanish into thin air?
