Shocking Discovery Beneath Mexico City: ‘Monkey Rats’ Emerge from Sewers, Baffling Scientists

In a chilling revelation, Mexican biologists have uncovered a grotesque new species prowling the labyrinthine sewers of Mexico City—a hybrid creature dubbed the ‘monkey rat.’

With eerily human-like hands, elongated limbs, and a prehensile tail, this nocturnal abomination defies taxonomic classification. ‘It’s an evolutionary anomaly,’ said Dr. Elena Vázquez, lead researcher.

Genetic tests confirm unprecedented DNA blending of rodents and primates, sparking theories of clandestine lab experiments or mutagenic pollution. Authorities warn residents to avoid drainage systems, citing the creatures’ aggressive behavior and potential disease vectors.
